🌸 Understanding India — Part 1
1'Satyam Shivam Sundaram' is the foundation of which Indian philosophy?
💡 Explanation
Satyam Shivam Sundaram (Truth, Goodness, Beauty) is a foundational concept in Vedanta philosophy, representing the ultimate nature of reality.
2Which is the oldest of the four Vedas?
💡 Explanation
The Rigveda is the oldest of the four Vedas, composed around 1500-1200 BCE, containing hymns dedicated to various deities.
3The Upanishads are mainly based on which subject?
💡 Explanation
The Upanishads focus on philosophy and self-knowledge, exploring concepts like Atman (soul) and Brahman (universal consciousness).
4Who is considered the author of the Mahabharata?
💡 Explanation
Sage Vyasa is traditionally credited as the author of the Mahabharata, one of the two major Sanskrit epics of ancient India.
5In which language was the "Ramayana" composed?
💡 Explanation
The Ramayana was composed in Sanskrit by Sage Valmiki, making it one of the earliest works in classical Sanskrit literature.
6Who discovered zero?
💡 Explanation
Aryabhata is widely credited with conceptualizing zero as a placeholder, a discovery that revolutionized mathematics.
7"Sarve Bhavantu Sukhinah" is taken from which text?
💡 Explanation
This peace mantra, meaning 'May all be happy', originates from the Vedas and reflects universal well-being.
8The major treatise of Ayurveda is —
💡 Explanation
Charaka Samhita, Ashtanga Hridaya, and Sushruta Samhita are all major classical treatises of Ayurveda, covering medicine and surgery.
9Where was Nalanda University located?
💡 Explanation
Nalanda University was located in present-day Bihar and was one of the world's first residential universities, attracting scholars globally.
10'Ahimsa Paramo Dharmah' is associated with —
💡 Explanation
This principle, meaning 'non-violence is the highest duty', is a core tenet of Jainism, emphasizing non-harm to all living beings.
11Where is the 'Triveni Sangam'?
💡 Explanation
The Triveni Sangam, the confluence of the Ganges, Yamuna, and mythical Saraswati rivers, is located in Prayagraj.
12Mohenjo-daro and Harappa were part of which civilization?
💡 Explanation
Mohenjo-daro and Harappa were major urban centers of the Indus Valley Civilization, one of the world's oldest urban cultures.
13Where was Mahatma Gandhi born?
💡 Explanation
Mahatma Gandhi was born on 2 October 1869 in Porbandar, a coastal town in present-day Gujarat.
14Who gave the teaching of the "Gita"?
💡 Explanation
Lord Krishna delivered the teachings of the Bhagavad Gita to Arjuna on the battlefield of Kurukshetra.
15The Ajanta and Ellora caves are in which state?
💡 Explanation
The Ajanta and Ellora cave complexes, famous for rock-cut architecture and paintings, are located in Maharashtra.
16Who wrote 'Sangeet Ratnakar'?
💡 Explanation
Sharangadeva authored Sangeet Ratnakar, a foundational Sanskrit text on Indian classical music and dance.
17Bhakti movement saint Kabir wrote in which language?
💡 Explanation
Kabir wrote in Sadhukkadi, a mixed dialect blending various regional languages, making his poetry widely accessible.
18Who is considered the author of the Panchatantra?
💡 Explanation
Vishnu Sharma is credited with writing the Panchatantra, a collection of animal fables teaching moral and political wisdom.
19Where was the "Panchayati Raj" system first implemented?
💡 Explanation
The Panchayati Raj system was first implemented in Rajasthan in 1959, in Nagaur district, as a model of local self-governance.
20What is India's national song?
💡 Explanation
Vande Mataram is India's national song, written by Bankim Chandra Chatterjee, while Jana Gana Mana is the national anthem.
21How many states does India have in total?
💡 Explanation
India currently has 28 states, along with 8 union territories, as per the reorganization following Jammu & Kashmir's 2019 status change.
22Who was the President of the Constituent Assembly?
💡 Explanation
Dr. Rajendra Prasad presided over the Constituent Assembly and later became independent India's first President.
23The Gupta period is known by which name?
💡 Explanation
The Gupta period (c. 320-550 CE) is called the Golden Age of India due to remarkable achievements in art, science, and literature.
24"Satyameva Jayate" is taken from which Upanishad?
💡 Explanation
This phrase, meaning 'Truth alone triumphs', is inscribed in India's national emblem and comes from the Mundaka Upanishad.
25The meaning of 'Himalaya' is —
💡 Explanation
'Himalaya' derives from Sanskrit — 'Hima' (snow) and 'Alaya' (abode), literally meaning 'abode of snow'.
26When was the 'Bharat Ratna' instituted?
💡 Explanation
The Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian award, was instituted in 1954 to honor exceptional service and achievement.
27Who received the first Bharat Ratna?

28In Indian culture, the 'Navratnas' were in whose court?
💡 Explanation
The Navratnas, nine legendary courtiers including Birbal and Tansen, adorned the court of Mughal Emperor Akbar.
29Where was "Satyagraha" first used?
💡 Explanation
Mahatma Gandhi first developed and used Satyagraha (non-violent resistance) in South Africa before bringing it to India.
30Where was the Buddha born?
💡 Explanation
Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha, was born in Lumbini, in present-day Nepal, around 563 BCE.
31Who was the last Tirthankara of Jainism?
💡 Explanation
Mahavira Swami was the 24th and last Tirthankara of Jainism, who spread the teachings of non-violence and truth.
32The Ajanta caves are famous for —
💡 Explanation
The Ajanta caves are renowned worldwide for their exquisite ancient Buddhist paintings depicting Jataka tales.
33Bharatanatyam dance is associated with which state?
💡 Explanation
Bharatanatyam, one of India's oldest classical dance forms, originated in the temples of Tamil Nadu.
34Kathakali dance belongs to which state?
💡 Explanation
Kathakali, known for its elaborate costumes and expressive storytelling, is a classical dance form native to Kerala.
35Mahabalipuram is famous for which art?
💡 Explanation
Mahabalipuram, a UNESCO World Heritage Site, is celebrated for its remarkable rock-cut sculptures and monuments.
36Who wrote the play 'Shakuntala'?
💡 Explanation
Kalidasa, the celebrated classical Sanskrit poet and playwright, wrote 'Abhijnanasakuntalam' (Shakuntala).
37What is India's national animal?
💡 Explanation
The Bengal Tiger is India's national animal, symbolizing strength, grace, and the country's rich biodiversity.
38What is India's national bird?

The Indian Peacock is the national bird, known for its vibrant plumage and cultural significance in Indian art and mythology.
39What is India's national flower?
💡 Explanation

40When did the Indian Constitution come into effect?

42Who gave the slogan "Jai Jawan Jai Kisan"?

43The "Incredible India" campaign is related to —
💡 Explanation
Incredible India is a major tourism promotion campaign launched by the Ministry of Tourism, Government of India.
44Who gave the slogan "Back to the Vedas"?
💡 Explanation
Swami Dayanand Saraswati, founder of the Arya Samaj, gave the slogan 'Back to the Vedas' promoting Vedic reform.
45Who designed the Indian national flag?
💡 Explanation
Pingali Venkayya designed the original tricolor flag, which was later adapted into India's official national flag.
46When was "Jana Gana Mana" sung for the first time?
💡 Explanation

47Who founded "Shantiniketan"?
💡 Explanation
Rabindranath Tagore founded Shantiniketan as an experimental educational institution emphasizing nature and creativity.
48"Dandiya" dance belongs to which state?
💡 Explanation
Dandiya Raas is a traditional folk dance from Gujarat, performed with sticks during the Navratri festival.

💡 Explanation

Subhas Chandra Bose popularized the slogan 'Jai Hind', which became a rallying cry for the Indian independence movement.
🌸 Understanding India — Part 2
51India's geographical name "India" is derived from which word?
💡 Explanation
The name 'India' derives from the Sindhu (Indus) River. Ancient Persians and Greeks referred to the region beyond the Indus as 'India'.
52Who gave India the name "Hindustan"?
💡 Explanation
The Mughals popularized the name 'Hindustan', meaning 'land of the Hindus/Indus', during their rule over the subcontinent.
53The name Jambudvipa is associated with —
💡 Explanation
Jambudvipa is an ancient name for the Indian subcontinent found in classical Hindu, Buddhist, and Jain cosmology and texts.
54From which direction did the Aryans arrive in India?
💡 Explanation
According to the widely accepted theory, the Aryans migrated into the Indian subcontinent from the north-west, through Central Asia.
55What did the Mughals contribute to Indian culture?
💡 Explanation
The Mughals made significant contributions across architecture (Taj Mahal), music (Hindustani classical traditions), and literature (Persian and Urdu works).
56In which article of the Constitution is the name "India" mentioned?
💡 Explanation
Article 1 of the Indian Constitution states: 'India, that is Bharat, shall be a Union of States'.
57Which era comes first in historical chronology?
💡 Explanation
The Prehistoric era, before written records existed, precedes the Ancient, Medieval, and Modern periods chronologically.
58The Harappan civilization belongs to which period?
💡 Explanation
The Harappan (Indus Valley) civilization flourished during the Bronze Age, roughly from 3300 to 1300 BCE.
59The 'Gurukul' system was what type of education system?
💡 Explanation
The Gurukul system was a traditional education method where students lived with their teacher (guru), learning through daily practice and dialogue.
60What was the 'Pathshala' known for?
💡 Explanation
Pathshalas were traditional Indian schools primarily known for imparting religious and moral education alongside basic literacy.
61The Madrasa education system is related to which religion?
💡 Explanation
Madrasas are educational institutions rooted in Islamic tradition, historically teaching religious studies along with other subjects.
62Which policy did the British government make for education in India?
💡 Explanation
Wood's Despatch of 1854 was a landmark British colonial policy that laid the foundation for a structured education system in India.
63What is the 1854 British education policy called?
💡 Explanation
The 1854 policy, known as Wood's Despatch, is often called the 'Magna Carta of English Education' in India.
64When was IIT established in the field of technical education?
💡 Explanation
The first Indian Institute of Technology (IIT Kharagpur) was established in 1951, marking a major step in India's technical education.
65What is the main objective of the National Education Policy 2020?
💡 Explanation
NEP 2020 emphasizes holistic, flexible, and multidisciplinary education aimed at the all-round development of learners.
66What is the role of teachers?
💡 Explanation
Teachers play a multifaceted role encompassing nation building, driving social reform, and instilling moral values in students.
67What was the main objective of the Education Policy of 1986?
💡 Explanation
The National Policy on Education 1986 focused on providing equal educational opportunities to all sections of society, especially the disadvantaged.
68When was independent India's first education policy made?
💡 Explanation
India's first National Policy on Education after independence was formulated in 1968, based on the Kothari Commission's recommendations.
69When did economic policy begin in independent India?
💡 Explanation
Independent India's structured economic planning began in 1950 with the establishment of the Planning Commission.
70When was the Planning Commission established?
💡 Explanation
The Planning Commission was established in 1950 to formulate India's Five Year Plans and oversee economic development.
71The Five Year Plans are related to —
💡 Explanation
The Five Year Plans were centralized economic programs designed to guide India's overall economic development.
72The agricultural revolution is related to which field?
💡 Explanation
The agricultural (Green) revolution focused on boosting grain production through improved seeds, irrigation, and farming techniques.
73Who is considered the father of the Green Revolution?
💡 Explanation
M. S. Swaminathan is celebrated as the father of India's Green Revolution for his pioneering work in agricultural science.
74When was NITI Aayog established?
💡 Explanation
NITI Aayog was established in 2015, replacing the Planning Commission, to serve as India's premier policy think tank.
75Where are the Directive Principles of State Policy mentioned in the Constitution?
💡 Explanation
The Directive Principles of State Policy are enshrined in Part IV of the Indian Constitution, guiding governance and welfare policies.
76Fundamental Rights are in which part of the Constitution?
💡 Explanation
Fundamental Rights, which guarantee basic civil liberties to citizens, are found in Part III of the Indian Constitution.
77When were the Fundamental Duties added?
💡 Explanation
Fundamental Duties were added to the Constitution in 1976 through the 42nd Amendment Act, inspired by the Constitution of the USSR.
78When did the drafting of the Constitution begin?
💡 Explanation
The Constituent Assembly began drafting the Indian Constitution in 1946, a process that took nearly three years to complete.
79When did the Indian Constitution come into effect?
💡 Explanation
The Constitution came into effect on 26 January 1950, chosen to honor the 1930 declaration of Purna Swaraj (complete independence).
80When was the Indian National Congress established?
💡 Explanation
The Indian National Congress was founded in 1885 by A.O. Hume, becoming the primary vehicle of India's independence movement.
81What is the revolt of 1857 called?
💡 Explanation
The 1857 revolt is known by multiple names — Sepoy Mutiny, First War of Independence, and an early anti-British freedom struggle.
82When did Gandhiji arrive in India?
💡 Explanation
Mahatma Gandhi returned to India from South Africa in January 1915, later becoming the leading figure of the independence movement.
83When did the Non-Cooperation Movement begin?
💡 Explanation
The Non-Cooperation Movement, led by Gandhi, began in 1920 as a mass campaign of peaceful resistance against British rule.
84When did the Salt Satyagraha begin?
💡 Explanation
The Salt Satyagraha, famously known as the Dandi March, began in 1930 as a protest against the British salt tax.
85In which year did the Quit India Movement begin?
💡 Explanation
The Quit India Movement was launched in August 1942, demanding an end to British rule and immediate independence for India.
86What is the meaning of secularism?
💡 Explanation
Secularism, as embodied in the Indian Constitution, means equal respect and treatment for all religions by the state.
87Who was the President of the Constituent Assembly?
💡 Explanation
Dr. Rajendra Prasad served as the President of the Constituent Assembly that drafted India's Constitution.
88Who was the maker/architect of the Constitution?
💡 Explanation
Dr. Bhimrao Ambedkar chaired the Drafting Committee and is widely regarded as the chief architect of the Indian Constitution.
89The Panchsheel principles were formed between which countries?
💡 Explanation
The Panchsheel (Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence) were agreed upon between India and China in 1954.
90When did the Non-Aligned Movement begin?
💡 Explanation
The Non-Aligned Movement was formally established in 1961, with India as one of its founding members, advocating neutrality during the Cold War.
91In which year did the Panchayati Raj system begin?
💡 Explanation
The Panchayati Raj system of local self-governance was formally recommended and began implementation starting in 1957.
92The 73rd Constitutional Amendment is related to —
💡 Explanation
The 73rd Constitutional Amendment (1992) gave constitutional status to Panchayati Raj institutions across India.
93When was the state of Jharkhand established?
💡 Explanation
Jharkhand was carved out of Bihar and established as a separate state on 15 November 2000.
94Who was the first Chief Minister of Jharkhand?
💡 Explanation
Babulal Marandi became the first Chief Minister of Jharkhand upon its formation in November 2000.
95Jharkhand is mainly famous for —
💡 Explanation
Jharkhand is renowned for its rich mineral wealth, particularly coal, and is often called the 'mineral hub' of India.
96What is the main language of the tribal community in Jharkhand?
💡 Explanation
Santhali is the primary language spoken by the Santhal tribal community, one of the largest tribal groups in Jharkhand.
97The PESA Act in Jharkhand is related to —
💡 Explanation
The PESA Act (Panchayats Extension to Scheduled Areas) empowers tribal self-governance in scheduled areas including Jharkhand.
98Who is the main pillar of 'democracy'?
💡 Explanation
In a democracy, the people are the fundamental pillar, as sovereignty and power ultimately rest with the citizens.
99What is the duty of citizens in a democracy?
💡 Explanation
Citizens in a democracy share multiple duties including voting, paying taxes, and abiding by the law to sustain civic order.
100Which word is NOT in the Preamble of the Indian Constitution?
