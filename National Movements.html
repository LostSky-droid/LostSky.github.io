📿 Section A — Brahmo Samaj & Raja Ram Mohan Roy
1Who is called the "Father of Modern India's Renaissance"?
💡 Explanation
Raja Ram Mohan Roy (1772–1833) is called the "Father of Modern India's Renaissance." He founded Brahmo Samaj (1828), fought against sati, illiteracy, and child marriage.
2Brahmo Sabha (later Brahmo Samaj) was founded in:
💡 Explanation
Brahmo Sabha was founded in 1828 by Raja Ram Mohan Roy, which later became Brahmo Samaj. Remember: Atmiya Sabha = 1814 (earlier) and Brahmo Samaj = 1828.
3Raja Ram Mohan Roy's first published work was:
💡 Explanation
Tuhfat-ul-Muwahhiddin (A Gift to Monotheists), published in 1803, was Roy's first work — it exposed irrational religious beliefs. Satyarth Prakash belongs to Dayananda Saraswati.
4Tuhfat-ul-Muwahhiddin was published in:
💡 Explanation
Tuhfat-ul-Muwahhiddin was published in 1803 — a full 25 years before Brahmo Samaj (1828). It was Roy's earliest major intellectual work against irrational religious practices.
5Atmiya Sabha was founded in:
💡 Explanation
Atmiya Sabha was founded in 1814 by Raja Ram Mohan Roy in Calcutta. It campaigned against idolatry, untouchability, caste rigidities, and intoxicants — predating Brahmo Samaj by 14 years.
6Raja Ram Mohan Roy supported which two educators in establishing Hindu College at Calcutta in 1817?
💡 Explanation
Raja Ram Mohan Roy supported David Hare and Alexander Duff in establishing Hindu College at Calcutta in 1817. Roy also established Vedanta College (1825) which combined Indian learning with Western sciences.
7Vedanta College was established by Raja Ram Mohan Roy in:
💡 Explanation
Vedanta College was established in 1825 by Raja Ram Mohan Roy. It combined Indian learning with Western sciences — a unique concept at that time. Hindu College was earlier in 1817.
8Native Marriage Act (1872) was enacted largely due to the work of:
💡 Explanation
Keshava Chandra Sen founded the Indian Reform Association (1870) which led to the Native Marriage Act 1872 — legalizing Brahmo marriages, fixing minimum marriage age, and allowing inter-caste marriage.
9Keshava Chandra Sen founded the Indian Reform Association in:
💡 Explanation
Keshava Chandra Sen founded the Indian Reform Association in 1870. This led to the Native Marriage Act of 1872. He also started The Indian Mirror in 1861. Remember: Association (1870) → Act (1872).
10Raja Ram Mohan Roy's lifespan was:
💡 Explanation
Raja Ram Mohan Roy lived from 1772 to 1833. The other dates: 1824–1883 = Dayananda Saraswati | 1820–1891 = Ishwar Chandra Vidyasagar | 1817–1898 = Sir Syed Ahmad Khan.
🕌 Section B — Wahabi & Deoband Movements
11Wahabi Movement was started by:
💡 Explanation
Wahabi Movement was started by Sayyid Ahmad of Rae Bareli around Patna, Bihar in 1820. It was a Sunni Islamic revivalist movement aimed at restoration of original Islam (Dar-ul-Islam).
12Wahabi Movement was one of the primary causes of:
💡 Explanation
Wahabi Movement was one of the primary causes of the Muslim uprising of 1857. It ultimately created isolationist and separatist tendencies among Indian Muslims and did NOT develop into a nationalist movement.
13Deoband Movement was organised by:
💡 Explanation
Mohammad Qasim Nanotavi and Rashid Ahmed Gangohi organised the Deoband Movement at Darul Uloom, Deoband, Saharanpur district, UP in 1866. Mahmud-ul-Hasan and Shibli Numani were important later leaders.
14Year of Deoband school establishment:
💡 Explanation
Deoband school was established in 1866 at Deoband, Saharanpur district, UP. Remember: Wahabi = 1820, Deoband = 1866, Satyashodhak Samaj = 1873, Triple 1875 movements come later.
15Aligarh Movement was Pro-British while Deoband Movement was:
💡 Explanation
Deoband was Anti-British — it aimed at moral and religious regeneration and keeping alive the spirit of jihad against foreign rulers. This is the KEY contrast: Aligarh (Pro-British, modern education) vs Deoband (Anti-British, religious regeneration).
16The twin objectives of Deoband Movement were:
💡 Explanation
The twin objectives of Deoband: (1) Propagate pure teachings of Quran and Hadis, and (2) Keep alive spirit of jihad against foreign rulers. Unlike Aligarh which focused on Western education and British-Muslim relations.
17Important leaders of Deoband Movement were:
💡 Explanation
Important leaders of Deoband Movement: Mahmud-ul-Hasan and Shibli Numani. The founders were Nanotavi and Gangohi (1866). Mahmud-ul-Hasan and Shibli Numani were prominent later leaders.
18Wahabi Movement ultimately created which kind of tendencies among Indian Muslims?
💡 Explanation
Wahabi Movement created isolationist and separatist tendencies among Indian Muslims. It did NOT develop into a nationalist movement — this is an important distinction from other social-religious reform movements.
19Wahabi Movement aimed at restoration of:
💡 Explanation
Wahabi Movement was a famous Sunni Islamic revivalist movement that aimed at restoration of the original spirit of Islam (Dar-ul-Islam). It sought to protect Islam from influence of Sikhs in Punjab and British in Bengal.
20Deoband school is located in which district?
💡 Explanation
Darul Uloom Deoband is located in Deoband, Saharanpur district, UP. Common trap: Aligarh = Aligarh Movement (Sir Syed Ahmad Khan) and Patna = Wahabi Movement (Sayyid Ahmad). Deoband = Saharanpur.
🌾 Section C — Satyashodhak Samaj & Phule
21The twin objectives of Deoband Movement were:
💡 Explanation
The twin objectives: (1) Propagate pure teachings of Quran and Hadis, (2) Keep alive spirit of jihad against foreign rulers. Unlike Aligarh which focused on Western education.
22Important leaders of Deoband Movement were:
💡 Explanation
Important later leaders of Deoband Movement: Mahmud-ul-Hasan and Shibli Numani. Founders (1866) were Nanotavi and Gangohi — don't confuse founders with important leaders.
23Aligarh Movement was Pro-British while Deoband Movement was:
💡 Explanation
Deoband was Anti-British — key contrast with Aligarh. Aligarh = Pro-British, modern education, Western outlook. Deoband = Anti-British, Islamic regeneration, spirit of jihad.
24Satyashodhak Samaj was founded by:
💡 Explanation
Jyotiba Phule founded Satyashodhak Samaj (Truth-Seekers' Society) in 1873. He also coined the term "Dalits" and was a champion against caste discrimination. Savitribai was his wife and first female teacher.
25Satyashodhak Samaj means:
💡 Explanation
Satyashodhak = Truth-Seekers' Society. "Satya" = Truth, "Shodhak" = Seeker/Searcher. Founded 1873 by Jyotiba Phule to fight Brahmin domination and caste oppression in Maharashtra.
26The term "Dalits" was first coined by:
💡 Explanation
Jyotiba Phule first coined the term "Dalits." Common trap: many students say Ambedkar — but Phule coined it earlier. Ambedkar later popularized and championed Dalit rights.
27Jyotiba Phule was Poona Municipality Commissioner between:
💡 Explanation
Jyotiba Phule served as Poona Municipality Commissioner from 1876 to 1883. He received the honorary title "Mahatma" in 1888 from Vithalrao Krishnaji Vandekar.
28Who gave Jyotiba Phule the honorary title "Mahatma" in 1888?
💡 Explanation
Vithalrao Krishnaji Vandekar gave Jyotiba Phule the title "Mahatma" in 1888. Note: Gandhi received the title "Mahatma" from Rabindranath Tagore — don't confuse the two!
29Savitribai Phule is considered the pioneer of:
💡 Explanation
Savitribai Phule is considered the pioneer of the feminist movement in India. She was the first female teacher, founded Mahila Seva Mandal (1852), and her work inspired Gandhi and Ambedkar.
30Savitribai Phule was the first:
💡 Explanation
Savitribai Phule was India's first female teacher. She and Jyotiba established the first Modern Girls' School in 1848 at Pune. By 1851, they had 3 schools in Pune.
31Modern Girls' School was established in:
💡 Explanation
Modern Girls' School was established in 1848 in Pune by Savitribai and Jyotiba Phule. Key dates: Girls' School = 1848, Mahila Seva Mandal = 1852, Satyashodhak Samaj = 1873.
32Mahila Seva Mandal was founded by Savitribai Phule in:
💡 Explanation
Mahila Seva Mandal was founded in 1852 by Savitribai Phule. Sequence: Girls' School 1848 → 3 schools by 1851 → Mahila Seva Mandal 1852 → Satyashodhak Samaj 1873.
33By 1851, Savitribai and Jyotiba had established how many schools in Pune?
💡 Explanation
By 1851, Savitribai and Jyotiba had established three schools in Pune. They started with the first Modern Girls' School in 1848 and rapidly expanded girls' education in Maharashtra.
34Savitribai Phule's work inspired which two leaders?
💡 Explanation
Savitribai Phule's work inspired Gandhi and Dr. B.R. Ambedkar. Her efforts in girls' education and anti-caste work laid the foundation for later independence and Dalit rights movements.
35Satyashodhak Samaj used which medium to convey anti-Brahmin messages?
💡 Explanation
Satyashodhak Samaj used Tamashas (folk dramas) and Ganpati festival to spread anti-Brahmin messages to common people. This mass-communication approach was very effective in rural Maharashtra.
⚔️ Section D — Singh Sabha
36Singh Sabha was founded at Amritsar on:
💡 Explanation
Singh Sabha was founded at Amritsar on October 1, 1873 by Thakur Singh Sandhawalia and Giani Gian Singh. It aimed at Sikh religious reform and revival of Sikh traditions.
37Singh Sabha was founded by:
💡 Explanation
Singh Sabha was founded by Thakur Singh Sandhawalia and Giani Gian Singh in 1873. It led to the establishment of Khalsa College (1892) and Khalsa Diwan at Lahore (1900).
38Khalsa College was founded at Amritsar in:
💡 Explanation
Khalsa College was founded at Amritsar in 1892. Sequence: Singh Sabha 1873 → Khalsa College 1892 → Khalsa Diwan at Lahore 1900. Don't mix up these three dates.
39Khalsa Diwan was established at Lahore in:
💡 Explanation
Khalsa Diwan was established at Lahore in 1900. Tip: Khalsa College (Amritsar) = 1892, Khalsa Diwan (Lahore) = 1900 — always remember city + year together.
40Khalsa College was established to promote:
💡 Explanation
Khalsa College was established to promote Gurumukhi language, Sikh learning, and Punjabi literature. The Singh Sabha movement focused on reviving Sikh identity, scripture, and regional language.
🔮 Section E — Theosophical Society
41Theosophical Society was founded by:
💡 Explanation
Theosophical Society was founded by Madame H.P. Blavatsky and Colonel M.S. Olcott in New York (1875). Annie Besant was a later prominent leader — not the founder.
42Theosophical Society was founded in:
💡 Explanation
Theosophical Society was founded in New York in 1875. Its HQ later moved to India (Adyar, near Madras) in 1882. Don't confuse founding location (NY) with Indian HQ (Adyar).
43Theosophical Society's HQ moved to India in:
💡 Explanation
Theosophical Society's HQ moved to India in 1882, specifically to Adyar near Madras (Chennai). Founded New York 1875 → Moved India 1882. The 7-year gap is a common exam question.
44Theosophical Society's Indian HQ was at:
💡 Explanation
Theosophical Society's Indian HQ was at Adyar, near Madras (Chennai). Adyar is a neighbourhood in Chennai — this is a very specific detail that often appears in exams.
45A prominent leader of the Theosophical Society was:
💡 Explanation
Annie Besant was the most prominent Indian leader of the Theosophical Society. She later also founded the Home Rule League (1916). She is a key figure connecting Theosophy with Indian nationalism.
46The motto of Theosophical Society was:
💡 Explanation
Theosophical Society's motto: "There is no religion higher than truth." Compare: Arya Samaj = "Back to the Vedas" | Gandhi = "Truth is God" | Theosophical Society = "No religion higher than truth."
47The main aim of Theosophical Society was:
💡 Explanation
Main aim of Theosophical Society: Comparative study of various religions, philosophies, and sciences. It was a universal, non-dogmatic approach — unlike Arya Samaj which focused specifically on Vedic Hinduism.
48Theosophical Society drew inspiration from:
💡 Explanation
Theosophical Society drew inspiration from Upanishads, Samkhya, Yoga, and Vedanta — the ancient Indian philosophical traditions. This is why it gained popularity in India despite being founded in New York.
🕌 Section F — Aligarh Movement & Arya Samaj
49Aligarh Movement was started by:
💡 Explanation
Sir Syed Ahmad Khan (1817–1898) started the Aligarh Movement. He founded MAO College at Aligarh in 1875 to promote modern Western education for Muslims. Pro-British approach.
50Sir Syed Ahmad Khan lived from:
💡 Explanation
Sir Syed Ahmad Khan: 1817–1898. Memory trick: 1817 = 1 year before Marx born (1818), 1898 = same year as Bismarck died. Compare: Dayananda = 1824–1883 | Ranade = 1842–1901.
51Muhammadan Anglo-Oriental College was established at Aligarh in:
💡 Explanation
MAO College was established in 1875 at Aligarh. The "Triple 1875" to remember: Arya Samaj (Bombay) = 1875, Theosophical Society (NY) = 1875, MAO College (Aligarh) = 1875!
52Aligarh Movement's approach was:
💡 Explanation
Aligarh Movement was Pro-British — Sir Syed believed Muslims should cooperate with British rule and gain modern education. Contrast: Deoband = Anti-British. This contrast is a favourite exam point.
53Arya Samaj was founded by:
💡 Explanation
Swami Dayananda Saraswati founded Arya Samaj in Bombay in 1875. His slogan was "Back to the Vedas." He was born in Tankara, Gujarat in 1824 and wrote the famous Satyarth Prakash.
54Arya Samaj was founded in which city and year:
💡 Explanation
Arya Samaj was founded in Bombay (Mumbai) in 1875. DAV College was later established in Lahore (1886). Triple 1875: Arya Samaj + MAO College + Theosophical Society — all in same year!
55Arya Samaj is based on two basic tenets:
💡 Explanation
Arya Samaj's two basic tenets: (1) Monotheism (belief in one God), (2) Infallible authority of the Vedas. Hence the slogan "Back to the Vedas" — Vedas are the final authority, not priests.
56Arya Samaj's famous slogan was:
💡 Explanation
Arya Samaj's famous slogan: "Back to the Vedas." Dayananda also gave another famous call: "India for Indians" (1876). "Truth is God" = Gandhi. "One Caste, One God" = Narayana Guru.
57Arya Samaj launched ___ movement to purify Hindus influenced by other religions:
💡 Explanation
Arya Samaj launched the Shuddhi movement to "purify" and reconvert Hindus who had converted to other religions. "Shuddhi" means purification. This was controversial but effective in retaining Hindu numbers.
58First DAV College was established at:
💡 Explanation
First DAV (Dayananda Anglo-Vedic) College was established at Lahore in 1886. Arya Samaj founded in Bombay 1875, but DAV College = Lahore 1886. Now there are 900+ DAV schools across India.
59Dayananda Saraswati was born in:
💡 Explanation
Dayananda Saraswati was born in Tankara, Gujarat in 1824. Though he founded Arya Samaj in Bombay, his birthplace was Tankara (now in Morbi district, Gujarat). Lifespan: 1824–1883.
60Dayananda Saraswati gave the call "India for Indians" in:
💡 Explanation
Dayananda gave the call "India for Indians" in 1876 — one year after founding Arya Samaj (1875). This was an early nationalist slogan predating the Indian National Congress (1885) by nearly a decade.
61Dayananda Saraswati's famous work was:
💡 Explanation
Satyarth Prakash (The True Exposition) = Dayananda Saraswati. Quick reference: Tuhfat = Roy | Hind Swaraj = Gandhi | Gita Rahasya = Tilak | Satyarth Prakash = Dayananda.
62Satyarth Prakash was first published in:
💡 Explanation
Satyarth Prakash was first published in 1875 — the same year Arya Samaj was founded. So Dayananda founded Arya Samaj AND published his masterwork in the same year 1875.
63Dayananda Saraswati propagated chaturvarna based on:
💡 Explanation
Dayananda's chaturvarna was based on occupation followed, NOT birth. This was a reform of the traditional birth-based caste system — he believed social divisions should be merit/work based, not hereditary.
64Dayananda Saraswati's birth year:
💡 Explanation
Dayananda Saraswati was born in 1824 (died 1883). Tip: 1772 = Roy, 1817 = Sir Syed, 1820 = Wahabi starts, 1824 = Dayananda born. Remembering birth years of all reformers is crucial for MCQs!
🙏 Section G — Prarthana Sabha & Govind Ranade
65Prarthana Sabha was established in Bombay in:
💡 Explanation
Prarthana Sabha was established in Bombay in 1867. It was formally organized by Dr. Atma Ram Pandurang and later Govind Ranade became its key figure. "Prarthana" = Prayer.
66Prarthana Sabha was formally founded by:
💡 Explanation
Prarthana Sabha was formally founded by Dr. Atma Ram Pandurang in 1867. Govind Ranade later became its most influential leader. Don't say Ranade founded it — he was the key leader, not founder.
67Govind Ranade was called:
💡 Explanation
Govind Ranade was called the "Socrates of Maharashtra". Titles to remember: Roy = Father of Renaissance | Lala Lajpat Rai = Punjab Kesari | Ranade = Socrates of Maharashtra.
68Govind Ranade lived from:
💡 Explanation
Govind Ranade: 1842–1901. Quick recall: 1824–1883 = Dayananda | 1817–1898 = Sir Syed | 1842–1901 = Ranade. He became Bombay High Court judge in 1893.
69Deccan Educational Society was founded by:
💡 Explanation
Deccan Educational Society was founded by Justice Govind Ranade. Ranade was multi-faceted: Prarthana Sabha leader, Deccan Educational Society founder, Bombay High Court judge, and "Socrates of Maharashtra."
70Govind Ranade became a judge at the Bombay High Court in:
💡 Explanation
Govind Ranade became judge at Bombay High Court in 1893. He lived 1842–1901, so he was a judge for the last 8 years of his life. Prarthana Sabha spread to South India by Kandukuri Veeresalingam.
🙏 Section G cont. + Section H — Ramakrishna Mission
71Prarthana Sabha was spread to southern India by:
💡 Explanation
Kandukuri Veeresalingam spread Prarthana Sabha to southern India. He is called the "Father of Telugu Renaissance" and worked on widow remarriage and women's education in Andhra.
72Ramakrishna Mission was founded by:
💡 Explanation
Swami Vivekananda founded Ramakrishna Mission in 1897. Ramakrishna Paramhansa was Vivekananda's guru, not the founder. Common trap — don't mix guru (Ramakrishna) with founder (Vivekananda).
73Ramakrishna Mission was founded in:
💡 Explanation
Ramakrishna Mission was founded in 1897. Memory: Vivekananda's famous Chicago speech = 1893 → Mission founded 4 years later = 1897. Located at Belur Math, West Bengal.
74Ramakrishna Mission is located at:
💡 Explanation
Ramakrishna Mission HQ is at Belur Math, West Bengal (near Kolkata). Don't say "Calcutta/Kolkata" — the specific answer expected is Belur Math. Similar trap: Theosophical = Adyar (not Madras).
75Ramakrishna Mission is also called:
💡 Explanation
Ramakrishna Mission is also called the Vedanta Movement. Vivekananda popularized Vedanta philosophy globally, especially through his 1893 Chicago speech. The mission is based on Vedantic principles.
76Motto of Ramakrishna Mission:
💡 Explanation
RK Mission motto: "Atmano Mokshartham Jagad Hitaya Cha" (For one's own liberation and the welfare of the world). Arya Samaj = Back to Vedas | Theosophical = No religion higher than truth.
77Swami Vivekananda was born as:
💡 Explanation
Swami Vivekananda was born as Narendranath Datta on January 12, 1863 in Calcutta. He later became a disciple of Ramakrishna Paramhansa and took the name Vivekananda.
78Swami Vivekananda was born on:
💡 Explanation
Vivekananda was born on January 12, 1863. This date is celebrated as National Youth Day. Don't confuse: Jan 26 = Republic Day | Jan 12 = Vivekananda/National Youth Day.
79Swami Vivekananda was born in:
💡 Explanation
Vivekananda was born in Calcutta (Kolkata). Belur Math is where the Ramakrishna Mission HQ is — his birthplace (Calcutta) ≠ Mission HQ (Belur Math). Both are in West Bengal though.
80Vivekananda's famous speech was at:
💡 Explanation
Vivekananda's famous speech was at the 1893 World's Parliament of Religions in Chicago. He began with "Sisters and Brothers of America" — putting India on the world philosophical map.
81January 12 is celebrated in India as:
💡 Explanation
January 12 = National Youth Day (Vivekananda's birthday). Key dates: Jan 12 = Vivekananda/Youth Day | Jan 26 = Republic Day | Aug 15 = Independence Day | Nov 26 = Constitution Day.
82Vivekananda's guru was:
💡 Explanation
Vivekananda's guru was Ramakrishna Paramhansa. The Mission is named after the guru (Ramakrishna), but was founded by the disciple (Vivekananda). The guru-shishya relationship is central to this movement.
⚖️ Section I — SNDP, Justice Party, Periyar & Others
83SNDP (Sree Narayana Dharma Paripalana Yogam) was founded by:
💡 Explanation
Narayana Guru founded SNDP (Sree Narayana Dharma Paripalana Yogam) in 1903 in Kerala. His famous slogan: "One Caste, One Religion, One God for All." He set up 40+ temples across Kerala.
